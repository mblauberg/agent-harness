# Legal decision overviews

Use this structure for a non-filing page that helps decision-makers understand
resolved and unresolved legal work. It organises source-bound legal information;
it does not supply professional advice or make the commercial decision.

For each issue, record only the fields the reader needs:

1. **Position or options:** the shared current position, proposed position, or
   genuinely open choices.
2. **Status:** agreed, proposed, undecided, or awaiting confirmation.
3. **Legal basis:** the legislation, authority or professional material that
   supports the entry, at the source's actual level of confidence.
4. **Decision required:** the choice still reserved to the decision-makers.
5. **Professional confirmation:** the legal point that still needs qualified
   confirmation, where applicable.

Use neutral shared-position wording unless individual attribution affects
authority. State positively what each source supports and what remains open.
Keep a limitation when it changes how the reader may rely on an entry; do not
repeat routine drafting history or caveats in every row. Silence, an inference
or unconfirmed material never becomes approval.
