# Family Violence And Redaction

Use this reference for safety-sensitive wording and filing-facing redaction checks. Use the family-law skill for substantive family-law procedure and current law.

## PPN And DVO Language

Use neutral document descriptions unless a source and verified issue frame support stronger wording:

- `Police Protection [Notice/Direction] dated [DATE]`.
- `the DVO application material`.
- `the affidavit affirmed on [DATE]`.
- `contemporaneous safety context`.
- `alleged family violence`.
- `safety and communication concerns`.

Do not write:

- `the DVO proves family violence`;
- `the Magistrates Court found family violence`;
- `the respondent is a perpetrator`;
- `coercive control was established`;
- `abuse of process` unless supported by law, evidence and the forum strategy.

Natural prose must not convert dated safety context into a finding, diagnosis, character conclusion or merits
finding. Keep the source status visible.

## Terminology Currency

- In Queensland, the Police Protection Notice (PPN) was replaced by the Police Protection Direction (PPD) for police-issued protections made from 1 January 2026.
- Use the term the actual instrument uses, taken from the document itself and its date. Verify the instrument type and issuing date against the source before naming it. Do not retro-label an earlier PPN as a PPD, or the reverse.
- This is a naming and source-discipline point only. It does not change the rule that a PPN, PPD or DVO is a dated document or safety context, not a judicial finding of family violence, unless another source proves that finding.

## Redaction Checks

Before filing or serving, check for:

- residential addresses;
- unsafe phone numbers or email addresses;
- employer details if safety-sensitive;
- bank account numbers;
- card numbers;
- medical details not needed for the application;
- names or identifying details of third parties where not needed;
- broad message histories where a targeted extract would suffice.

## Proportionality

- File targeted extracts, not whole sensitive histories, unless the evidence-use and human-authority gates are both satisfied.
- If family-violence material is relevant to property, safety or pre-action issues, explain the relevance.
- Keep graphic, medical, reproductive-health and third-party material out unless necessary and settled.

## Forum Fit

- QCAT: use safety context only where relevant to hearing participation, service, confidentiality, procedural fairness or stay prejudice.
- Magistrates Court: use safety context only where relevant to enforcement, service, urgency or prejudice.
- FCFCOA: use safety context for risk, communication, pre-action steps, disclosure safety, property considerations and procedural management, subject to source, redaction and proportionality gates.

## Cross-Proceeding Safety Consistency

When related proceedings raise the same safety concern, keep the safety
objective consistent but draft each request for that forum. Do not transplant
another forum's terminology, powers, service rules or proposed orders.

- Verify the current power, form, service requirement and confidentiality
  process from that forum's official sources.
- Ask only for directions that are necessary and proportionate to the evidence
  and the proceeding.
- Minimise residential, contact, employment, financial, health and third-party
  details, subject to the applicable disclosure and filing rules.
- Keep served and unredacted registry material separate where the forum permits
  or directs that approach. Never assume a redaction process is available.
- Use dated safety material as context, not as a finding, unless an authorised
  source proves the finding.
- Put any unresolved disclosure, service or protective-direction choice through
  the project's human-authority and current-law verification gates.
