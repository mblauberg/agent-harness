# Forum And Document Recipes

Use this reference after loading the relevant forum skill.

## QCAT And QCATA

Style:

- Plain tribunal prose.
- Short headings and ground-by-ground logic.
- Explain enough for a tribunal member to see the issue quickly.
- Keep orders, evidence and submissions separate.

Common documents:

- Form 39 or appeal submissions: decision, route, timing, grounds, error type, materiality, relief.
- Form 44 stay submissions: order to be stayed, appeal status, urgency, arguable case, prejudice, balance of convenience, conditions.
- Timing material: delay, explanation, prejudice, merits, interests of justice, proposed timetable.
- Calculation appendix: preserve competing figures; do not silently normalise amounts.

## Queensland Magistrates Court

Style:

- Most form-bound and order-first.
- Use exact UCPR form labels and party labels.
- Keep affidavit evidence mechanical and chronological.
- Put only relevant cross-proceeding material in the filing.

Common documents:

- Form 9 application: relief sought, short grounds, urgency or listing request, supporting material.
- Form 46 affidavit: identity, purpose, chronology, current issue, exhibits, prejudice, final link to orders.
- Form 47 exhibit certificate: match the affidavit and exhibit label exactly.
- Exhibit bundle: certificate, optional cover, source pages, no internal material.

## FCFCOA

Style:

- Safety-sensitive, proportionate and carefully qualified.
- Use family-law terminology only after checking the current family-law skill.
- Keep property, safety and QCAT overlap distinct.
- Do not turn QCAT debt findings into final FCFCOA property conclusions.

Common documents:

- Initiating Application extract: precise and brief numbered orders, no argument.
- Genuine Steps Certificate extract: tick only what is true and support safety or urgency claims.
- PPP Financial Summary extract: use verified values, `NK` only where the form permits and the status is true.
- Affidavit extract: first-person facts, no opinion unless admissible and source-backed, annex only proportionate material.
- Urgent listing letter: practical request, urgency, filed material, safe contact details.

## Cross-Proceeding Writing

- Say which forum can grant which relief.
- Do not ask one forum to do another forum's job.
- Use cross-proceeding material only to explain relevance, urgency, enforcement, safety or overlap.
- Mark estoppel, Anshun, jurisdictional gateway, leave and prospects issues for source-backed analysis and user instruction.

## Orders And Minutes

An order is a command the court or tribunal has power to make and will enforce. Draft the shortest set that obtains the relief, and put each operative order in the document that owns it. Test every proposed order against the list below; if it fails, cut it or move the point to submissions. A lean minute reads as the operative relief (time, stay, the substantive order) plus at most one or two genuinely necessary procedural orders and a single liberty to apply; everything else dilutes the key relief and invites attack.

Cut an order that:

- **Restates the forum's own power or duty back to it.** Cite the power once in the submission and apply it; do not order the tribunal to exercise a power it plainly has, or recite its own directions to it.
- **Restates what the registry or court does automatically.** If the rules, the form or the directions already provide for issuing, sealing or serving the order (for example, "sealed copies be served by email"), do not seek an order for it. But where service method, an abridgment of time, deemed or substituted service, or safe contact arrangements are genuinely in dispute, keep an express order: that is relief, not routine.
- **Restates a duty the party already owes** (for example, "the applicant prosecute the appeal expeditiously and comply with directions"). That is the rules' baseline, not relief; it reads as a self-imposed burden and invites a breach or costs allegation.
- **Duplicates another order** (for example, "notify any change of email address" once an email-for-service direction is already sought; or the same relief stated in a directions list and again in a form's dedicated section). Ask for each item of relief once, in its proper home. For a non-publication order, use the form's dedicated non-publication section, cross-referred once from the directions list.
- **Concerns another forum the court cannot control.** Do not order, or "save", what happens in a different proceeding. A saving such as "these orders do not affect proceeding X" both over-reaches and can be read as conceding the order does not reach X. Explain the cross-forum position in the submission instead.
- **Volunteers a self-imposed burden or condition the forum has not required** (for example, a non-dissipation undertaking or an offer to disclose changes in available funds). It is a self-represented-litigant trap: it hands the other side a monitoring and breach hook, and on a money stay it cuts against a party who is resisting that very disclosure elsewhere. The correct vehicle is a liberty to be heard before any condition or security is imposed, not a standing self-imposed order.
- **States "no order as to costs" where no costs order is available, or where the only recoverable items are fixed by the rules and are not being sought.** Rely on the default rule. In a costs-capped jurisdiction, do not cut a permitted filing, e-filing, service or search-fee order merely because broader legal costs are unavailable.
- **"Grants" a co-filed application from within another application's minute.** It is circular: co-filed applications are listed and decided together, and each carries its own orders. Do not order that the other application "be granted".

Distinguish a true default from useful relief: affirmatively *seeking* an order to crystallise a defeasible default (for example, an on-the-papers determination a party could otherwise apply to convert to an oral hearing) is legitimate where it locks in a position, often for safety. Seek it once, in the application that owns it; do not also restate it in a consolidated minute.

Consolidate and match:

- **One consolidated orders minute per wave;** the submissions point to it rather than carry order lists (QCAT/QCATA worked example: `qcat-minor-civil-dispute/references/document-structures.md`, *Orders and minutes: drafting discipline*). Collapse multiple liberty-to-apply orders into one general liberty. A standalone contingent liberty (for example, safe-participation directions "if an oral hearing is listed") can signal that you expect the contingency you are arguing against; fold it into the general liberty plus the substantive submission.
- **An order's wording must not exceed or contradict what a sworn affidavit deposes the application seeks.** Where the affidavit is already sworn, align the order to it (or re-swear); do not narrow or broaden an order into a mismatch with the sworn record.
- **Ground the basis of a discretionary order in the documented source, not an unrelied claim**: anchor a safety or non-publication order on the annexed safety material, not on a medical condition the proceeding does not rely on.
- **Verify the rendered title and order numbering as well as the source.** A templated cover or title block can drift from the document's real title; the title must read identically in every place it appears.

## Written Advocacy Rhythm

For each issue in submissions:

1. State the proposition or issue.
2. Identify the rule, authority, order, direction or legal threshold.
3. Apply the source-anchored facts.
4. State the consequence for the relief sought.

Do not bury that sequence inside narrative history. If the paragraph is mainly background, check whether it belongs in a chronology, affidavit or annexure instead.

For affidavits:

- Use first-person facts, dates and documents.
- Do not argue from the facts inside the affidavit.
- Move evaluative language, legal conclusions and relief consequences to submissions.

For correspondence:

- Use `legal-correspondence-style.md`.
- State the request, deadline, attachments and practical next step before background.
- If the email is longer than a screen, check whether it should be a short cover email with an attached letter or outline.

For a speaking outline (oral hearing support):

- Build it from the filed submissions, not alongside them: the headings plus the first sentence of each
  paragraph are the outline. Do not write a second, divergent argument.
- One page per ground where possible: the contention, the two or three record pinpoints to take the bench to,
  and the answer to the expected question against.
- Oral argument is generally confined to the issues in the written outline; a point with no written trace
  reads as afterthought. Add nothing new orally without flagging it as new and explaining why.
- Write it to be glanced at, not read: short lines, no full sentences except quoted text, exact page/paragraph
  pinpoints for everything to be read aloud.

## Affidavit Drafting

Affidavits prove facts within the deponent's knowledge. Argument, relief and legal characterisation belong in submissions or orders.

For paragraph numbering, sub-paragraph hierarchy, headings, bolding and amount formatting across all three
forums, follow the consolidated rules in `australian-english-house-style.md` (*Headings, Numbering And
Document Structure*); that file is the single owner of the shape.

Naming:

- First reference to each person: full name, then a bracketed short form and relationship, e.g. `the respondent, [FULL NAME] ('[SHORT FORM]')` or `the respondent's parent, [FULL NAME] ('[SHORT FORM]')`. Use the short form consistently after that; do not re-introduce the full name later.
- Introduce at the first mention, not the first convenient paragraph. The deponent is not introduced in the third person; the title and `I am the applicant` establish identity.
- Where two people share a surname, distinguish by given name or defined short form, and keep it consistent.

Facts, not opinion or diagnosis:

- The deponent may swear their own experience as fact (for example, `I felt panicked and at times could not speak or think clearly`). Do not self-diagnose a clinical condition (`trauma response`, `PTSD`) where the affidavit does not rely on medical evidence; describe the experience in lay terms.
- Do not argue from the facts, renew or seek relief (`I renew that request in this proceeding`), or characterise another court's or tribunal's order as wrong (`is an error`, `cannot be calculated`). State the fact and its status instead: `disputed and not admitted`, `under appeal`, `I have been unable to reconcile it`.

Producibility and reserves:

- State producibility once, as a blanket in the purpose section (`Where I refer to a document, message or record I have not annexed ... I can produce it on disclosure if required`). Do not append `can be produced if required` to individual paragraphs.
- Keep deliberate conditional reserves that hold back sensitive detail for proportionality (`particulars are available if the family violence is put in issue`). Those are purposeful, not fluff.
- Do not annex a selective extract of the other party's court filing merely because it is adverse to them. Identify the proceeding, filing date and paragraph/page, and call it an admission only where that is legally accurate. If the document is already before the same forum, refer to that record. If it must be proved in this proceeding, annex or exhibit the authenticated record needed for the point, with enough context for fairness. A selective extract may invite a completeness/context response, uses an annexure slot, and carries the other party's contrary characterisation. Reserve annexures for documents needed to prove the issue, especially authoritative court records such as sealed decisions or issued directions; use the blanket producibility statement only for non-essential referred documents.

Scope and proportionality:

- Lead with a strong, dated, source-anchored example rather than over-particularising one weak incident, especially where stronger examples are held in reserve.
- Before relying on a prior hearing or decision, check the official record: raising it can invite the other party to cite an adverse finding sitting on the same material. Include it only if the gain outweighs that, or it explains and neutralises the adverse point.
- Keep cross-proceeding material as procedural and disputed-liability context; do not invite the new forum to re-decide another forum's question (collateral attack), particularly where that question is under appeal elsewhere.
- Mind the forum page cap (FCFCOA r 5.08 Division 2 affidavit body: 10 pages, cover and jurat excluded). Verify by render; cut duplicated history and repeated reserves before seeking leave for length.
- If paragraph numbers are cross-referenced in the text, adding or removing a paragraph shifts every later number. Prefer sentence-level edits, or fix every cross-reference.

## Forms Of Address

- QCAT and QCATA: a member is addressed as `Member [surname]`, not `Your Honour`. In writing, refer to `the Tribunal` or `the Appeal Tribunal`. `QCATA` is a citation shorthand for the Appeal Tribunal, not its statutory name.
- Queensland Magistrates Court: a magistrate is addressed as `Your Honour` (spoken) and referred to as `the Magistrate` or `the Court` in writing.
- FCFCOA: a judge is `Your Honour` (spoken) and `the Honourable Justice [surname]` or `the Court` in writing; a senior judicial registrar or judicial registrar is referred to by title, not `Your Honour`.
- Spell honorifics the Australian way: `Honourable`, not the American `Honorable`. Do not use `Esq.`.

## Document Function Table

| Document | Function | Style |
|---|---|---|
| Orders | Commands sought from the court or tribunal | Short, numbered, precise |
| Submission | Legal argument from facts and law to relief | Structured, sourced, restrained |
| Affidavit | Evidence of facts | First person, numbered, factual |
| Chronology | Event map | Date, event, source, relevance |
| Solicitor note | Advice request or risk analysis | Internal, candid, source-cited |
| Correspondence | Practical communication | Clear request, deadline, next step |
| Annexure index | Evidence navigation | Labels, titles, dates, page counts, source |
